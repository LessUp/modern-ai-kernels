# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary readers are senior open-source developers, technical interviewers, and systems-minded learners who evaluate repositories quickly and skeptically. They arrive to judge engineering depth, architectural clarity, benchmark credibility, and whether the project teaches something real beyond source code volume.

## Product Purpose

TensorCraft-HPC's public site should function as a technical whitepaper, architecture showcase, and project academy for modern AI kernels. Its job is to turn the repository into an evaluable technical artifact: readers should understand the system model, optimization path, benchmark evidence, and research context before or alongside reading code.

## Brand Personality

Scholarly, industrial, uncompromising. The site should feel like a lab notebook refined into a public artifact: precise, evidence-led, quietly ambitious, and visually memorable without becoming startup-marketing theater.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic documentation homepages, safe SaaS card grids, glassmorphism, neon-on-black "AI product" clichés, and magazine-style serif/mono editorial pastiche. Avoid decorative gradients, empty hero metrics, vague claims, and any diagram, icon, or illustration that becomes unreadable in one color theme.

## Design Principles

1. Evidence first. Every visual and content decision should help readers verify architecture, performance posture, or implementation judgment.
2. Architecture is the narrative. The site should guide readers from system model to operator detail instead of presenting pages as isolated docs.
3. Diagrams are first-class assets. Charts, flow diagrams, and operator maps should carry explanatory weight rather than decorate prose.
4. Dark-led, light-safe. The visual system may be optimized for a focused technical-reading scene, but every asset and component must remain equally legible in light mode.
5. One bilingual artifact. English and Simplified Chinese should mirror the same information architecture and professional standard.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast or better across both themes. Preserve readable line lengths, keyboard-accessible navigation, visible focus states, and reduced visual noise in dense technical pages. Motion should remain subtle and optional, and diagrams must not depend on color alone to communicate hierarchy or state.
